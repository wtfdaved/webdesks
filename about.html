The Story Behind WebDesks

WebDesks started as a personal project born out of frustration. Every day at the desk meant jumping between a dozen different browser tabs — one for a timer, one for a color picker, one for ambient music, another to convert a video. Each tool asked for an account or put the good stuff behind a subscription wall.

The idea was simple: build a single tab that replaced all of them. A Pomodoro timer that actually worked offline. A PDF merger that didn't upload files to strangers' servers. A screen recorder that didn't require a Chrome extension.

What started as a handful of tools grew into a full suite as more people asked for features. Focus Town — a pixel art town that grows with your completed focus sessions — was added when we realised productivity tools shouldn't feel like work to use. The Audio Mixer followed when we noticed how many people were looking for white noise, brown noise, and lo-fi combined.

Today WebDesks has 21 tools and is used by students, developers, writers, designers, and remote workers who want their tools to stay out of the way and just work. The project remains independent, ad-supported, and completely free.
