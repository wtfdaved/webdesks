3. Data Handling by Tool

Because your privacy matters, here is how each major tool category handles your data:

📄 PDF Merger

Your PDF files are processed entirely within your browser using the open-source pdf-lib JavaScript library. Your files are never uploaded to any server. The merged PDF is generated locally and downloaded directly to your device. We do not access, log, or retain any information about the documents you merge.

🎥 Screen Recorder

Screen and audio recording uses your browser's built-in MediaRecorder API. Your recording is captured and stored temporarily in your browser's memory (RAM) and is never transmitted to any server.

Your responsibility: You are solely responsible for ensuring you have the legal right to record any screen content, including obtaining consent from others who may be visible or audible in your recordings. Do not use the Screen Recorder to record content in violation of applicable copyright law, privacy law, or third-party terms of service.

🎬 Media Converter (WebM to MP4)

Video and image conversion is performed entirely in your browser using FFmpeg.wasm. Your media files are never uploaded to any server. Conversion happens locally on your device's CPU and may use significant processing resources.

📝 Tasks, Notes, Board & Scratchpad

Your tasks, notes, kanban board cards, scratchpad content, and preferences are stored in your browser's localStorage. This data never leaves your device. It is not accessible to us and persists until you clear your browser's site data. We strongly recommend exporting or backing up important notes regularly as localStorage can be cleared by browser updates or privacy tools.

🎮 Focus Town

Focus Town progress, town buildings, and gamification data are stored in your browser's localStorage. No data is sent to our servers. Clearing your browser data will reset your Focus Town progress.

🎵 Audio Mixer & White Noise Generator

All audio is generated and processed locally in your browser using the Web Audio API. No audio data is transmitted or recorded by WebDesks. Your volume presets and mixer settings are saved in localStorage on your device.
